# Samsung-Supply-Chain-Analysis-Power-BI
**Project Overview**

This project analyzes Samsung’s supply chain performance using Power BI and Power Query to evaluate supplier efficiency, inventory management, shipment reliability, and sales performance. The goal is to identify operational bottlenecks, cost drivers, and opportunities to improve supply chain efficiency and profitability.

**Business Problem**

Samsung’s supply-chain data is distributed across multiple operational areas such as suppliers, inventory, shipments, and sales. Without a centralized analytical view, it becomes difficult to monitor delays, costs, stock health, and profitability across the supply chain.

**Objective**

The project aims to:

Evaluate supplier performance and lead times

Monitor inventory levels and stock trends

Analyze shipment delays and logistics performance

Track revenue and profit across sales channels

Identify cost drivers and operational risks

**Tools & Technologies**

Power BI – Data visualization and dashboard creation

Power Query – Data transformation and cleaning

Data Modeling – Star schema design

DAX – KPI calculations and metrics

**Dataset Description**

The dataset contains supply chain and sales transaction data covering a one-year period.

Key dimensions include:

Supplier

Product

Inventory

Carrier

Customer

Date

**Key measures include:**

Revenue

Profit

Order Quantity

Unit Cost

Inventory Stock

Delay Quantity

**Data Preparation**

Data preparation involved several steps:

Removing duplicate records

Handling missing values

Standardizing categorical fields

Creating date hierarchies (Month and Year)

Engineering KPIs such as Profit Margin, Delay %, and Defect Rate

A star schema model was implemented to improve query performance and analytical efficiency.

**Dashboard Insights**

**Supplier Analysis**

Samsung Vietnam supplies the highest order quantity.

Suppliers from Taiwan show the highest quality scores.

India and Vietnam demonstrate faster lead times.

**Inventory Analysis**

S24 Ultra maintains the highest inventory levels.

Inventory peaks in October due to pre-festive demand.

Defect rates slightly increase during peak inventory months.

**Shipment Analysis**

Most shipments are delivered successfully.

Delays are primarily linked to carrier capacity issues.

Shipment costs increase during peak season, especially in December.

**Sales Performance**

Amazon generates the highest revenue.

Online channels show the fastest growth.

Wearables experience higher discounts due to promotions.

**Key Findings**

Strong overall revenue and stable profit margins.

Reliable supplier network with efficient lead times.

Shipment delays and logistics costs increase during peak seasons.

High-demand products face occasional delivery delays.

**Recommendations**

Improve demand forecasting for peak seasons.

Diversify logistics carriers to reduce dependency risks.

Strengthen quality inspections during high-inventory months.

Optimize procurement and discount strategies.

**Project Outcome**

The dashboard provides a comprehensive, data-driven view of Samsung’s supply chain operations, enabling better strategic decisions related to logistics, inventory planning, supplier management, and sales performance.
